Various Perspectives on Feminism
✖

Mrs. Blake

As a popular topic in conversation and society today, feminism takes on many definitions. Even established dictionaries often disagree. Some claim it is, “the theory of the political, economic, and social equality of the sexes,” and others claim that it is, “organized activity in support of women's rights and interests.” With the release of the viral NYC Catcall Video, music by artists like Beyoncé, and TED Talks by speakers like Chimamanda Ngozi Adiche, feminism has become a widely discussed but also controversial matter. What does feminism mean to those who are passionate about the cause? What are feminist activists doing to change the world today?

Wading Through the Asian Mystique
✖

Mrs. Kim

It has been claimed that the years 2014 and 2015 were banner years for Asian Americans in television as both the number of starring roles for Asian Americans and behind the scenes positions held by Asian Americans have increased. This workshop will investigate the validity of this statement by exploring the character roles of Asian Americans and placing the discussion within the larger historical context of Asian American portrayal in the media. Together, workshop participants will explore the Asian stereotypes of the dragon lady, geisha girl, and emasculated Asian male which have dominated American media for decades, and define for themselves what successful diversity in the media looks like.

Affirmative Action and College Acceptance
✖

Ms. Belkin & Mr. Kaser

Affirmative action is defined as the encouragement of women and the minority-group members, especially in employment or higher education. The policy in 1961 was designed to create a balanced diversity in the workforce and in schools by not taking any regard for race, gender, etc. when accepting or employing an individual. It has evolved into a set of laws to help ensure that women and minorities gain equal-footing in the world and to eradicate racism and sexism. However some argue that the policy has done just the opposite and caused discrimination. This workshop will explain how affirmative action works in depth, specifically in the college acceptance area.
